Website Experience Research

Creating an Impactful First Impression

Overview

As a research and analyst intern for Paradoxes, a market research company, I had an opportunity to research ways a website can engage clients.

Context

4 week project during a summer 2019 internship

Team of 2 with a mentor

Role

Project manager

UX researcher

Background

Founded in 2007, Paradoxes has been helping business and tech companies make data-driven sales, marketing, and product decisions. It prides itself on delivering quick, credible recommendations. Although the company developed strong relationships with its clients over the years, it struggled to expand its network as most people heard of Paradoxes through word of mouth.

Our research question

How can Paradoxes create an engaging, frictionless experience that will capture a potential client’s attention?

How did we analyze the problem?

Research breakdown & insights

+ Journey Map

The website’s intended audience were people in associate-level positions looking for a market research consulting agency. With no budget and a 4-week timeframe, my team found it difficult to conduct interviews with the intended audience. We interviewed 4 internal members holding those roles instead and they described their everyday life involving a new business to business (B2B) interaction. We aimed to identify our user’s goals, attitudes, and emotions when approaching a new or unfamiliar company.

After analyzing our notes, we identified 4 pain points:

  • limited time - There's multiple projects to work on and thoroughly analyzing a website isn't a priority.
  • vauge wording - "Terms like 'industry leader' don't mean anything if everyone uses it."
  • confusing navigation - Broken links, vague wording, and an unfamiliar website layout aren't user-friendly
  • questionable credibility - Has this company sucessfully made an impact? Are they trustworthy?

We integrated the pain points into a map to visualize the user's journey to research into multiple websites.

These findings led us to conclude that web searches are cumbersome and frustrating, and our website may be no exception.
