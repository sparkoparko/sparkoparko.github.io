Website Experience Research

Creating an Impactful First Impression

Overview

As a research and analyst intern for Paradoxes, a market research company, I had an opportunity to research ways a website can engage clients.

Context

4 week project during a summer 2019 internship

Team of 2 with a mentor

Role

UX researcher

Background

Founded in 2007, Paradoxes has been helping business and tech companies make data-driven sales, marketing, and product decisions. It prides itself on delivering quick, credible recommendations. Although the company developed strong relationships with its clients over the years, it struggled to expand its network as most people heard of Paradoxes through word of mouth.

Our research question

How can Paradoxes create an engaging, frictionless experience that will capture a potential client’s attention?

How did we analyze the problem?

Research breakdown

+ Journey Map

The website’s intended audience were people in associate-level positions looking for a market research consulting agency. With no budget and a 4-week timeframe, my team found it difficult to conduct interviews with the intended audience. We interviewed 4 internal members holding those roles instead and they described their everyday life involving a new business to business (B2B) interaction. We aimed to identify our user’s goals, attitudes, and emotions when approaching a new or unfamiliar company.

After analyzing our notes, we discovered that users are searching for the company's services, products, and their respective prices. The three pain points prevent them from efficiently collecting information:

  • limited time - There's multiple projects to work on and thoroughly analyzing a website isn't a priority.
  • confusing navigation - Vague wording and an unfamiliar website layout aren't user-friendly
  • questionable credibility - Has this company sucessfully made an impact? Are they trustworthy?

We integrated the pain points into a map to visualize the user's journey to research into multiple websites.

These findings led us to conclude that web searches are necessary, yet cumbersome and frustrating. Users appreciate it when content is easy to find.

+ Competitive analysis

In addition to the interview, we analyzed 24 competitors that conducted market research to identify website features that addressed the issues above. The competitors were segmented by similar size, audiences and services, and proximity to Seattle.

Finding #1: Distinctive website layout. We noticed that companies similar in size (i.e. Audienz, Mozaic Group, and Prime8 Consulting) directs the user to notice the company's solutions, mission statement, then its specific services. This layout optimizes our user's workflow, as it quickly directs them to all the necessary information.

[insert layout graphic]

Finding #2: Responsive design. The competitive analysis led us to believe that Paradoxes' website has lots of potential. Companies of similar size, audience, and services were starting to implement web design that adjusted according to the user's device. Inconsistencies over different mobile platforms can contribute to navigation problems.

Finding #3: Common features. Competitors implemented several features to market themselves as a responsible, trustworthy partner. Common features were a list of awards, clients, a contact and feedback form, and a social media presence.

What I've learned and moving forward

Reflections

In one month, I learned how to set project milestones and a final deadline. I am more familiar with my pace of work and how I work in a business setting, which was less structured than an academic one. I learned to value the data collection stage, as I found myself referring to it instead of drawing assumptions from my beliefs.

If I were to repeat this project again, I would have advocated for the company to value the human-centered design process more. The website design changed halfway in August and while it integrated some of our findings, our research was not done. Also, our interviewees were users within the company. Our findings may be biased and not accurately reflect the thoughts of our true users. In the future, it would be interesting to collaborate with a designer.
